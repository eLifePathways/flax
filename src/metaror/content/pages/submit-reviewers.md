MetaResearch Open Review (MetaROR) is extending an invitation to the academic community to join our platform as peer reviewers. Your expertise can significantly contribute to advancing scholarly communication through our Publish, Review, Curate model. As part of our commitment to transparency and constructive feedback, MetaROR utilises an open peer review system. Below, we outline the guidelines for our review process and invite you to become an integral part of this innovative approach to academic publishing.

## Open Peer Review in MetaROR

At MetaROR, we believe in the power of openness and dialogue. Our open peer review process is designed to enhance the credibility and accessibility of academic research. Reviews are published alongside the preprints, allowing for a transparent evaluation process that enriches the scholarly discourse.

## Signing Reviews: A Step Towards Transparency

Transparency and Accountability: We encourage reviewers to sign their reviews, acknowledging their contributions and fostering a transparent review culture.
Option for Anonymity: Recognising the diversity of preferences within our community, reviewers may also choose to submit their feedback anonymously. This flexibility supports a comfortable and accommodating review environment for all participants.

## DOI Assignment to Reviews

Each review submitted to MetaROR is assigned a Digital Object Identifier (DOI), underscoring the importance and recognisability of your scholarly contribution. This practice not only facilitates the citation of your review but also enhances its visibility within the academic community.

## Becoming a Reviewer for MetaROR

Your role as a reviewer is critical in guiding authors to refine their work and in upholding the standards of quality and integrity in research. Here are the steps to join MetaROR as a reviewer:

- Expert Evaluation: Employ your expertise to assess manuscripts, providing detailed and constructive feedback to authors.
- Commitment to Fairness: Approach each review with impartiality, ensuring an unbiased and ethical evaluation.
- Contribution to Scholarly Communication: By participating in the open review process, you contribute to the ongoing dialogue around research findings, enhancing the collective knowledge base.
